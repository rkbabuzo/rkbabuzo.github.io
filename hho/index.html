PROJECT

HealthyHempOil Website Revamp Proposal

 

This proposal describes potential problems of the current website including its possible solutions. This also includes the reconceptualization of the website's interface.

01

Target Audience:

HealthyHempOil’s target audience consists of retired or soon-to-retire 40 to 60-years old, male and female, experiencing chronic pain from various ailments. These men and women want to do business with a company that they can trust and help them find the right product for their pain.

 

They might not be entirely tech savvy and they might not have a whole lot of experience in purchasing products online.

 

Older people spend more time to understand pages, scan the text, and extract the information. Their slower and more measured approach to computers doesn’t always gain them better results. Some are much more likely to turn to search engines to complete tasks.

02

Potential Design Problems:

- Users won't be able to quickly find specific information. Generic website design is indistiguishable from other competitors.

- Lack of design elements that promote user engagement.

 

 

03

PROJECT Objectives:

  1. Promote user engagement and boost website credibility through attractive branded imagery and well-organized content.
  2. Provide an exceptional user experience through simple interface, intuitive navigation, and supportive design elements.
  3. Help users easily make purchasing decision from the information provided on healthyhempoil.com–without referring to other websites and search engines.

 

Strategies & ACTION STEPS:

1. Provide easily-discoverable social proof and CBD-related information:

  • Make social media and customer review link more noticeable
  • Add a “how to”, "help" or a “guide” links and banner images to the home page
  • Add engaging content to the website (infographics, tutorials, promo/discount banners, etc.)

2. Create a strong brand presence:

  • Utilize up-to-date website design trends and principles.
  • Use photos and images that convey an obvious connection to the content. Avoid cheesy or generic-looking stock photos.
  • Create relevant seasonal and product banner images that promote content.

3. Make users feel more secure:

  • Utilize graphic elements such as security and guarantee badges in the homepage. This includes the secure payment, money back guarantee, and GMP logos.
  • Make key product features prominent, this includes “100% Natural”, “Tested by…”, and  GMP logo.

LANDING PAGE

Seasonal Banner Images: These are just some samples from Canva. Final images are more detailed and content specific.

Banner Images: The purpose of these images is to show real and relevant content, not just to decorate the homepage.

  • 3 Slider images includes product photos, promos, etc. (e.g. product photos placed in a scene with some graphic elements added).
  • Seasonal banner images (summer, thanksgiving, Christmas, etc), may include promos
  • Banner occupies around 55% of the screen height.

 

Navigation:

  • Make navigation menu contents stretch to browser width.
  • Remove dropdown from Contacts, instead replace with CONTACT+FAQ. This makes the link more attractive since it explicitly describes the information that users would receive. Also makes FAQ easier to discover.

Infographics/Visual Content:

  • Illustration/graphic of the how-to-buy guide.
  • Link to an in-depth guide.
  • Utilize this space for visual content that promotes brand recognition.

Featured Products:

  • Slider images of featured products
  • Utilize graphic elements, typography, and special features to make the product look more credible.

Product Description:

  • Summary of valuable product information such as benefits, uses and application, etc.

Customer Reviews:

  • Easily-discoverable social proof boosts website's credibility.
  • May also include images of people with real connection to the company. This includes customers and those from the HHO team.

Footer:

  • Users tend to scroll down to the bottom of the page while the website is loading. Placing the links at the right column makes it easier for users to find useful links.
  • Add copyright information, security badges, useful links, etc.

RESOURCE PAGE

Sidebar:

  • Remove sidebar from resource pages to make it less cluttered and allow users to focus on more important information.
  • Remove sidebar to allow more space for images

Infographics and Graphics:

  • One column is better in accommodating compelling and attractive visual content– better images encourages brand recognition
  • Makes large blocks of text easier to read

Paragraph:

  • Narrow width compared to images.
  • Apply golden ratio for width, line height, and font size.

BLOGS PAGE

Blogs:

  • With smaller blog images, it would be easier to scroll through blogs posts and find relevant information.
  • Remove titles from blog images (use non-generic photos. Photos from Unsplash, Pexels, etc. can be used.)

Infographic:

  • Create pages for infographics, add short copy/description–useful for time-poor visitors.
  • Links from social media direct to these pages. Builds both inbound and internal links.

LEARN MORE PAGE

Learn More Tabbed Links:

  • Users should be able to visit the Learn More link directly since it already describes the information they would receive.
  • Retain dropdown page links

Learn More Tabbed Links:

  • Users can directly click the Learn More link
  • Information and links are easily discoverable and more organized.
  • Navigating and finding information is more easier

Related Links:

  • Can be blog posts, infographics, reviews, products, etc.
